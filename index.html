Carmo is an asset that we have to cherish, take care of,and see what we will do by the end of the month

Written by: Johney Brickman, Paul Benson, Daniel Wolf
  • 0

In an interview with the newspaper 'O Jogo', the vice-president of FC Porto, Vítor Baía, addressed various topics, from the January transfer market to the club's elections, including disciplinary issues related to players like David Carmo.

"If it had been our captain, Pepe, putting his head in front of an assistant's forehead, everything would have been messed up" Vítor Baía highlighted the difficulties of the winter market, classifying it as 'very ungrateful'. The Porto director stated that the club already has analyzes carried out and targets defined, but admitted the complexities and competitiveness to guarantee reinforcements.

Baía assured that, if it is not possible to sign a contract, the team led by Sérgio Conceição will face the challenges with the current squad, highlighting the confidence in the quality of the available players.
"It is very difficult to manage assets because they are all in their clubs and the good ones have their price. We have done the analysis, the targets defined and we will see if it is possible to reinforce the team", began by saying the former Portuguese international, quoted by 'Rádio Renascença'.

As for the case of David Carmo, Baía explained that the relegation to team B, led by António Folha, is related to the club's internal rules, group dynamics and specific situations in the locker room. "It has to do with the internal rules of the club, the working group, the coach himself, the day-to-day modus operandi", observed the director. "Carmo is in a competitive context, although on the B team, but he is an asset that we have to value, take care of and see what we will do by the end of the month", he explains.
